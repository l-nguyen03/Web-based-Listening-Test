What to evaluate?

Evaluate the pressurised feeling in your ear (e.g like when you are in an ascending/descending airplane) on a scale from -5 to 5 when you are listening to the samples. Pressurised here can also be described informally as feeling your ear being blocked, clogged or muffled

  • -5: The pressurised effect got significantly worse.
  • 5: The pressurised feeling completely disappears.
  • 0: Nothing changed compare to the reference.

How to interact with the interface?

  • Use the number keys on your keyboard (0 - maximum number of samples) to select a sample.
  • Key number 0 corresponds to the reference sample, which always have an evaluation of 0.
  • After you've chosen a sample, the corresponding audio will be played and looped (replayed automatically after it ends), the corresponding label will changed color so you can keep track of what you are listening to.
  • You can only control the slider corresponding to the audio you are listening to.
  • You can only use the arrow up and arrow down key on your keyboard to move the slider.

How to compare between samples?

Supposed you've listened to Sample #1 and your first impression is to give it an evaluation of 5. Then you listen to sample #2 and you feel that the effect in sample #2 disappear even more and maybe that should be a 5, you should then switch back and fourth between 1 and 2 to assign each an appropriate value. Repeat this process to each new samples that you hear to get a more correct impression instead of relying solely on your first impression.
