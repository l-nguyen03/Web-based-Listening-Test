Some important notices before taking the tests

How to interact with the testing interface?

  • You will always be presented with several audio samples and 1 reference sample (reference sample always has an evaluation of 0).
  • Each of the audio samples can only be played by pressing the corresponding number key, e.g. sample #1 can be played by using key number 1 on the keyboard.
  • Reference sample can be played by pressing the number key 0.
  • After choosing an audio sample, you can control its slider by pressing the key arrow up and down.
  • The audio sample is looped so it will replay automatically.
  • Each sample has a transition (from Noise-Cancelling off to Noise-Cancelling on), you should evaluate your listening sensation at and after this transition (the Noise-Cancelling on portion).
  • Remember to compare each sample with each other to get correct impressions of all samples by switching back-and-fourth between them, pay attention to the sensation after the transition and set the slider to the desired value.
  • Example: Supposed you've listened to Sample #1 and your first impression is to give it an evaluation of 7. Then you listen to sample #2 and you feel that the effect in sample #2 is way more obvious and maybe that should be a 7, you should then switch back and fourth between 1 and 2 to assign each an appropriate value. Repeat this process to each new samples that you hear to get a more correct impression instead of relying solely on your first impression.
